About Me

This is me

I'm currently a PhD candidate at the University of California - Davis, under the advisement of Dr. S. J. Ben Yoo.

I received my BEng degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Birla Institute of Technology - Mesra, India in 2012. Moving to the US to start my graduate school journey, I graduated with my MS degree in 2014 from the University of California - Irvine. After spending some time in research and consulting roles, I returned to graduate school for my PhD in 2017, where I am currently working at the Next Generation Networking and Computing Systems Laboratory.

I previously worked with the ARIANNA Neutrino Detector Group at UC Irvine, developing sensor systems for neutrino detection. My current research interests focus on electronic-photonic co-simulation, codesign and integration with applications in high performance computing and data centers.

I'm a very social person, who loves to spend time with his friends. In my spare time I'm often found playing games or on a roadtrip with my friends travelling and exploring the world around me. My hobbies include reading, learning something new, hiking, camping, martial arts, and sports.
